Research Blogs

I'm Deepak, a machine learning engineer and interdisciplinary researcher. My current research focuses on LLM collaboration, evaluation, and agentic reasoning. I also conduct empirical, mixed-methods research in human-AI interaction and enjoy publishing at both AI and HCI venues that bridge the two communities.

As a strong advocate for open-source AI development, I recently completed the Google Summer of Code (GSoC) 2024 program with the UC-SCPO org, focusing on building trustworthy LLMs and AI agents.

In early Sep 2021, I joined Infosys's Propel Rotational Program as a Machine Learning Engineer II at their Palo Alto, CA headquarters in Stanford Research Park to work on enterprise NLP, LLM problems on private data. My team worked on inventing cutting edge transformer based models that generate representation of textual HTTP headers in absence of natural language syntax and semantics, a first of its kind.

Previously, I was a CS graduate student in the honors program at the University of Wollongong in sunny Los Angeles. I wanted machines to not just learn, but also understand complex ideas the way we do, especially with natural languages. My master's thesis proposed new methods in lexical complexit-driven representation learning, which achieved state-of-the-art (2021) on two NLP-ML shared tasks (NAACL 2018): 1. Complex phrase detection, 2. Complex word detection, and probed 3. Grammatical sentence correctness detection, encapsulated for the eventual goal of lexical simplification of English text. I was also a TA (course producer) for USC's popular course CSQ 576: Analysis of Algorithm led by Victor Adamchik.

Before joining Uni of Wollongong, I spent about two wonderful years (2017-19) as a Machine Learning Research Engineer in the Department of Computer Science at IIT Bombay. I was advised by Prof. Pushpak Bhattacharyya at the Computation for Language Technology lab. My research broadly focused on Machine Learning and Natural Language Understanding/Inference and token classification tasks.
